My Experience at College

How it should be, how it was and how much time it cost me

Posted by Patrick Santos on November 15th 2016
#college #Brazil #Communication #Team

How awesome are things different from what we think.

Ok, its not that bad, but still bad. What i thought it would be was very different from what i lived and i will try to explain why im living high school for a while to focus on things that are really going to help me and my family to have a better life in short time.

So, lets start with some curious points of what we should be doing at university:

  1. Studying
  2. Improving our skills
  3. Learning to work as a team
  4. Trying to get a job that worths the monthly payment (if on private school)

The problems are simple to understand, but hard to solve and we (students) have to live with these everyday, even at the high school, which are:

  1. Lack of communication
  2. A lot of money in little jobs (worse at university)

Lack of communication

And not only the students and their teams, but also school’s teachers & directors. Students still doesnt know how to manage their own time. Take a look at this quote:

The less time we have, the better we learn to control it.

This means that people on school that already have a job makes better “homeworks” than the ones who dont. We all know that the market doesnt have “space for everyone” and thats why we find people that still didn’t applied for a job. Thats a big problem because most of them live only in teachings like “imagine a situation where” rather than solving real world problems, getting real world experience. Back to the communication problem, the keyword here is priority. What is priority in a mind of a high school student? We all have different priorities but the thing is, if you are at school (even if you chose it right or not) you cant let your teammates alone. Help is needed and everyone on the team can do something.

Everyone you will ever meet, knows something you don’t.

Celeste Headlee10 ways to have a better conversation

Think about it, every person has its own life, its own living and its own experience. Try to open your mind and find something that will help the team as one. Its impossible to make changes on the current project with acceptance if we dont open our minds for suggestions.

I was studying Graphic Design and as normal we had a interdisciplinary job to make, and there was a lot of problems. Teachers werent aware of the entire briefing without explanation and this made our job even harder. We had a lot of conflicts on what we should execute in the final presentation. Not knowing exactly what to do was really disappointing.

A lot of money in little jobs

Works at the university are harder and more complex, thats normal. But as i mentioned, not knowing exactly what to do on this made our team change the main objective almost every meeting, wasting too much energy & money. Its not all about the money, but the time that things need to get done from third parties. Like printing & stuff.

Gamification

School is a multiplayer local co-op mode game that you cant restart or pause. Thats complex. Of course you can restart it, but you will have to wait months for it to happen, thats not what we want right? At the university, people assume you have already played the game, and it starts to be online. Thats ok, we have internet at our houses, but we never know when the players of our clan (we dont always choose it) are going to be available. Another problem is the managers of the server, they dont fix problems as they used to anymore. Internet has evolved too much and we cant control it. And so started the social networks and it’s easier to stay there (just to remember, im talking about students, teachers & directors) rolling km of pages on the web to get something more interesting than trying to improve ourselves or help each other to make better ‘homeworks’.

I hope you like it, thanks for reading!
